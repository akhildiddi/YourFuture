Industrial Training Institute

Summary Of ITI :-

Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) are vocational training institutes in India that provide technical education and skill training to students. The courses offered by ITIs cover a wide range of trades and skills, such as electrician, fitter, welder, machinist, and plumber, among others. The courses are designed to provide practical training to students and equip them with the skills and knowledge required to succeed in the job market. ITIs are run by the Directorate General of Training (DGT), under the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship. The duration of courses varies from 6 months to 2 years, depending on the trade and level of course. After completion of the course, students can take the All India Trade Test (AITT) conducted by the National Council for Vocational Training (NCVT) to receive their certification.The main aim of ITIs is to develop a skilled workforce to meet the needs of the country's growing economy. ITIs also provide a platform for students to start their own businesses or become self-employed. The government of India has been taking various initiatives to promote vocational education and skill development through ITIs, such as the Skill India program, which aims to train over 400 million people by 2022.
