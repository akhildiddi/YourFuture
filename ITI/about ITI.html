ITI stands for Industrial Training Institute, which is a type of vocational education institute in India. These
institutes provide training in various industrial trades such as electrical, mechanical, electronics, and
welding, among others. The aim of these institutes is to provide practical and hands-on training to students in
the relevant trade so that they are equipped with the necessary skills to secure employment in their chosen
field.
The history of ITIs in India dates back to the 1950s when the government realized the need to provide practical
training to young people to help them secure employment in various industries. The first ITI was established in
1956 in Kolar Gold Fields, Karnataka, and since then, the network of ITIs has expanded rapidly across the
country. Today, there are over 15,000 ITIs in India, offering training to millions of students every year.

The curriculum of ITIs is designed to align with the requirements of industries and is updated regularly to keep
pace with the changing job market. Additionally, the government has taken several initiatives to improve the
standard of ITIs and make them more accessible to students from all backgrounds. Despite these efforts, however,
the quality of training in ITIs remains inconsistent across different states and institutes. Nevertheless, ITIs
continue to play an important role in providing technical education and employment opportunities to young people
in India.
